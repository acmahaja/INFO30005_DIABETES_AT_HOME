A short summary about diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

Diabetes is a complex condition which arises in patients whose body is unable to maintain healthy levels of glucose, a form of sugar and the main source of energy for our bodies, due to an inability of their bodies to produce sufficient amounts of insulin. Insulin is a hormone released by the pancreas, and which is essential in the process of converting sugars in the bloodstream into energy for our bodies to use. Without sufficient amounts of the hormone, glucose levels in the blood may rise to unhealthy levels and this may cause multiple symptoms and complications in the short and long term. Diabetes cannot be cured, however it is a condition which can be effectively managed with daily care and attention, often without severely impacting a person’s quality of life.

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is a condition in which the body develops a resistance to the normal effects of insulin, and eventually becomes unable to produce enough amounts of insulin, or becomes too resistant to its effects, to maintain healthy glucose levels in the blood.

This type of diabetes has strong genetic and family-related risk factors, and is also associated with a person’s lifestyle choices. The condition may be slowed down or even halted in people by improving their lifestyle choices and increasing physical activity.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes is a type of diabetes which may arise in women during pregnancy. The condition usually disappears after the baby is born, however some women will continue experiencing higher levels of glucose in the blood. In the latter case, it may develop into Type 2 diabetes.

Summary

Complications arising from diabetes may cause reduction in quality of life and life expectancy and, for the case of type 1 diabetes, symptoms may be sudden and potentially life-threatening.

With that being said, the condition can however be effectively managed and negative repercussions minimized with a mix of medical attention, daily care and a healthy lifestyle. Nowadays more often than not patients who get treated for diabetes are able to live out a normal life without having to worry about the more severe symptoms of the condition.
